# Olesia Brand Voice

Use this skill for content work connected to this therapist website or adjacent brand materials.

## Core voice

Write like a psychologist who is:

- warm
- empathetic
- emotionally intelligent
- grounded
- non-shaming
- practical
- softly authoritative
- more human than corporate

The voice should feel safe, relational, and trustworthy. It should not sound salesy, cold, inflated, or generic.

## Brand impression to preserve

The writing should create the sense of a therapist who:

- understands anxiety, self-criticism, burnout, and relationship pain from both professional knowledge and lived experience
- helps readers feel seen without dramatizing their pain
- explains emotions clearly and calmly
- offers practical next steps without sounding mechanical
- values self-compassion, emotional safety, and close relationships

## Writing style rules

- Prefer clarity over cleverness.
- Prefer warmth over polish, but keep the prose clean.
- Use direct address when helpful.
- Make the reader feel accompanied, not managed.
- Combine emotional validation with practical guidance.
- Use concrete examples, scenarios, or recognizable situations whenever possible.
- Keep structure visible with short sections, lists, subheads, or steps when the topic is instructional.
- Allow occasional light humor only when it lowers defensiveness and still feels respectful.

## Tone controls

Aim for this balance:

- warm, not overly sentimental
- professional, not clinical-cold
- expert, not superior
- calm, not flat
- hopeful, not vague
- encouraging, not pushy

## Universal guidance

### Ukrainian

Treat Ukrainian as the strongest reference voice.

- Preserve emotional nuance and natural rhythm.
- Sound human, intimate, and psychologically precise.
- Gentle phrases like invitations, reassurance, and collaborative framing are welcome.
- Avoid stiff institutional phrasing.

### English

Apply the same voice standard in English.

- Do not translate literally from Ukrainian.
- Rewrite so it sounds originally written in natural English.
- Keep the warmth, but make the phrasing idiomatic and clean.
- Use the newer long-form English article quality bar: composed, structured, calm, and clear.
- Avoid awkward phrases like "I am a human being" when a more natural equivalent exists.

## What strong copy looks like here

Strong copy for this brand usually includes:

- an emotionally recognizable opening
- a clear explanation of what the person may be experiencing
- a grounded psychological reframe
- one practical next step, framework, or invitation
- a calm CTA that respects the reader's pace

## What to avoid

Avoid:

- generic therapy cliches without specifics
- inflated promises
- abstract reassurance with no practical value
- dense paragraphs with no examples
- harsh, corrective, or moralizing language
- overly corporate or SEO-stuffed phrasing
- placeholder copy or unfinished FAQ-style text

Be careful with lines like "everything you need is already inside you." Use only if the surrounding copy adds specificity and depth.

## Conversion guidance

When writing pages intended to convert visitors into inquiries or bookings:

- keep the warmth, but add concrete clarity
- explain what working together is like
- reduce uncertainty around first contact or first session
- show who the service is for
- foreground practical fit, not just inspiration
- build trust before asking for action

Good conversion copy for this brand answers:

- What kind of struggles can be brought here?
- What happens in a session?
- What kind of approach does this psychologist use?
- Why might a client feel safe here?
- What shift or outcome is realistically possible?

## Trust vs conversion

Optimize both, but distinguish them:

- Trust is built through specificity, emotional accuracy, steadiness, and evidence of expertise.
- Conversion is improved through clarity, structure, fit language, and reducing hesitation around booking.

Do not trade away warmth for persuasion. Instead, make the next step feel safe and clear.

## Consistency checks

Before finalizing, check whether the draft sounds consistent with the brand:

- Does it sound like the same author across homepage, services, and articles?
- Is the tone warm and grounded rather than generic?
- Is the quality at least as strong as the better Ukrainian pages?
- If not in Ukrainian, does it preserve the personality rather than only the meaning?
- Do CTA and transitions match the calm, respectful voice?

## Editing workflow

When editing existing copy:

1. Keep the original emotional intent if it fits the brand.
2. Remove stiffness, vagueness, and repetition.
3. Add specificity where trust or clarity is weak.
4. Make process, fit, or outcomes clearer on service pages.
5. Preserve tenderness and non-judgment.

## Output standard

The final text should feel:

- warm
- clear
- emotionally safe
- practically useful
- trustworthy
- gently differentiated

If choosing between "more poetic" and "more useful," choose useful with grace.
